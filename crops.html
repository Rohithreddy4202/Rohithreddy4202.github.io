Shifting cultivation is a mode of farming long followed in the humid tropics of Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and South America. In the practice of “slash and burn”, farmers would cut the native vegetation and burn it, then plant crops in the exposed, ash-fertilized soil for two or three seasons in succession.more

Crop rotation is defined as a “system of growing different kinds of crops in recurrent succession on the same land” (Martin et al., 1976). Top 5 Most Profitable Crops in India – You Can Grow To Make Money

Many profitable major crops grown in India. Due to this, there has been a lot of improvement in the farming sector. These types of crops in India include food crops and raw material. In the below section, we show a list of crops grown in India. So, check out the below.

  1. RICE

    2. The first crop on the list is rice. Rice is a highly consumed food, and that is why the demand for this crop is very high. That is the reason it widely cultivated across the World. India holds the second position in producing rice. And covers one-third of the total cultivated Indian area.

    • It is a Kharif crop and staple food which widely consumed by half of the Indian peoples
    • It requires a temperature of 22-32℃ with high humidity.
    • Rice crops are commonly grown in 150-300 cm rainfall.
    • Deep clayey and loamy soil is suitable for rice crops.
    • West Bengal, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, and Bihar are the largest rice-producing Indian states.

  2. WHEAT

    3. Wheat is the second crop mentioned in this list. It is a rabi crop and staple food. After rice, wheat is the food crop that is widely consumed in India. It requires a low temperature and cools the growing season.

    • It requires a temperature of 10-15°C (Sowing) and 21-26°C (Harvesting) with bright sunlight.
    • Suitable rainfall for wheat production is around 75-100 cm.
    • Well-drained fertile loamy and clayey loamy are perfect for wheat crops.
    • in wheat production, India is in the second position.
    • Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Haryana, and Rajasthan are some central wheat-producing states in India.

  3. MAIZE

    4. The next profitable crop on the list is maize. Maize is the crop that used as both fodder and food. It is a Kharif crop that is popular among the people. After rice and wheat, maize is the only crop that everyone consumes.

    • Maize crop India requires a temperature of 21°C – 27°C for good growth. Also, high rainfall is suitable for this crop.
    • It can easily grow in old alluvial soil.
    • India is in the 7th position in the production of maize.
    • High-yielding variety seeds, fertilizers and irrigation are the main reason for increasing maize production.
    • Karnataka, Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, and Telangana are the top maize producing states.

  4. pulses

    5. The next crop on the list is pulses. This crop is a main source of protein. The most popular pulses which are grown in India are Tur (Arhar), urad, moong, Masur, peas and gram.

    • It requires a temperature of 10-15°C (Sowing) and 21-26°C (Harvesting) with bright sunlight.
    • Suitable rainfall for wheat production is around 75-100 cm.
    • Well-drained fertile loamy and clayey loamy are perfect for wheat crops.
    • in wheat production, India is in the second position.
    • Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Haryana, and Rajasthan are some central wheat-producing states in India.

  5. JUTE

    6. The last profitable crop on the list is Jute. It used to make burlap, mats, ropes, yarn, carpets, hessian or gunny cloths. Jute is popular as golden fibre. It is one of the best cash crops in India.

    • It is a Kharif crop and staple food which widely consumed by half of the Indian peoples
    • It requires a temperature of 22-32℃ with high humidity.
    • Rice crops are commonly grown in 150-300 cm rainfall.
    • Deep clayey and loamy soil is suitable for rice crops.
    • West Bengal, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, and Bihar are the largest rice-producing Indian states.
